Rarity

There are 8 rarities in the game.

Junk | Poor | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Epic | Legendary | Unique


As rarities increase, the items get stronger. Rarity Uncommon and above adds an extra random Enchantment.

Uncommon rarity adds up to 1 new enchantment

Rare rarity adds up to 2 new enchantments

Epic rarity adds up to 3 new enchantments

Legendary rarity adds up to 4 new enchantments

Unique rarity adds up to 5 new enchantments

Stat Randomization

Not only are the enchantments a gear piece is imbued with randomized, but some of it's base stats are also randomized.

In general, only the damage values on weapons and the armor rating on armors are variable.

As such, 2 gear pieces of the same rarity can have different base stats.

There are some exceptions like the Lightfoot Boots, these also have randomized movement speed values based on rarity.

For example, a Epic rarity Armet can have a 36 ~ 38 Armor Rating, which means that stat can roll between 36 to 38.


Weapon Properties

Most melee weapons operate on a Sweet/Sour Spot system, meaning hitting an enemy with the tip of the weapon will deal the most damage and hitting further down on the blade/haft will deal less damage. The number of sour spots vary between weapons.

Some weapons also have special properties, for example, the Castillion Dagger's and the Stiletto Dagger's 3rd hit deals 50% more damage.

The Felling Axe's attack can be chained infinitely as long as the attack doesn't miss or get's interrupted.

The Zweihander's secondary move set is also special since it's a 2-Hit attack, meaning 1 right click does a 2-Hit attack animation.

The "Combo" column reflects a weapon's attack animation during it's attack string, this description will be different to the in-game one, as those reflect the attack's coded damage type.

Melee Damage types are not yet implemented in the game, therefore, to avoid confusion, it's been altered to describe the attack animation.

As for the Weapon's reach/hitbox" column, weapon's length doesn't necessarily determine a weapon's reach, more so the weapon's hitbox length, due to different attack animations, shorter weapons could have better reach than longer weapons.

The radius value simply determines the radius of the cylindrical shape of a weapon's hitboxes, so the bigger the radius, the wider the hitbox and the easier it is to hit targets.
