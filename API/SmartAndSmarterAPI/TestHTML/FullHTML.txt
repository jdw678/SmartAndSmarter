																											<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><br />
In Dark and Darker, Weapons are a significant aspect of gameplay that require strategic consideration as players are given the opportunity to discover and utilize different types of weapons to suit their playstyle and preferences. In addition to the standard melee weapons like swords, maces, daggers, polearms, and axes, there are also ranged weapons like bows and crossbows that allow for combat from a distance. The game also features magical weapons that can be used to cast spells, as well as shields that can be used for defense.
</p><p>Weapons have different properties and parameters that affect their performance in combat, including attack damage, attack speed, range, attack animations. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rarity">Rarity</span></h2>
<p>Weapons can improve and become stronger as their rarity level increases. A weapon's rarity typically reflects its level of power, with higher rarity weapons providing more damage output and other attributes that can enhance the player's performance in combat.
</p><p>There are 8 rarities in the game.
</p><p><span class="colorrarity0">Junk</span> | <span class="colorrarity1">Poor</span> | <span class="colorrarity2">Common</span> | <span class="colorrarity3">Uncommon </span> | <span class="colorrarity4">Rare</span> | <span class="colorrarity5">Epic</span> | <span class="colorrarity6">Legendary</span> | <span class="colorrarity7">Unique</span>
</p><p>Rarity <span class="colorrarity3">Uncommon</span> and above adds an extra random <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">Enchantment</a>.
</p><p><span class="colorrarity3">Uncommon </span> rarity adds up to 1 new  <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantment</a>
</p><p><span class="colorrarity4">Rare</span> rarity adds up to 2 new  <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantments</a>
</p><p><span class="colorrarity5">Epic</span> rarity adds up to 3 new  <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantments</a>
</p><p><span class="colorrarity6">Legendary</span> rarity adds up to 4 new  <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantments</a>
</p><p><span class="colorrarity7">Unique</span> rarity adds up to 5 new  <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantments</a>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stat_Randomization">Stat Randomization</span></h2>
<p>Not only are the <a href="/Enchantments" title="Enchantments">enchantments</a> a gear piece is imbued with randomized, but some of it's base stats are also randomized.
</p><p>In general, only the damage values on weapons and the armor rating on armors are variable.
</p><p>As such, 2 gear pieces of the same rarity can have different base stats.
</p><p>There are some exceptions like the Lightfoot Boots, these also have randomized movement speed values based on rarity. 
</p><p>For example, a <span class="colorrarity5">Epic</span> rarity Armet can have a 36 ~ 38 Armor Rating, which means that stat can roll between 36 to 38.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weapon_Properties">Weapon Properties</span></h2>
<p>Some weapons also have special properties, for example, the Castillion Dagger's and the Stiletto Dagger's 3rd hit deals 40% more damage.
</p><p>The Felling Axe's attack can be chained infinitely as long as the attack doesn't miss or get's interrupted.
</p><p>The Zweihander's secondary move set is also special since it's a 2-Hit attack, meaning 1 right click does a 2-Hit attack animation.
</p><p>The "Combo" column reflects a weapon's attack animation during it's attack string, this description will be the in-game one, as those reflect the attack's coded damage type.
</p><p>Melee Damage types such as "Thrust, Slash, Pierce" are not yet implemented in the game.
</p><p>As for the Weapon's reach/hitbox" column, weapon's length doesn't necessarily determine a weapon's reach, more so the weapon's hitbox length, due to different attack animations, some shorter weapons could have better reach than longer weapons.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_Zones">Impact Zones</span></h2>
<p>Most melee weapons operate on a impact zones system, meaning hitting an enemy with the tip of the weapon will deal the most damage and hitting further down on the blade/haft will deal less damage.
The number of impact spots vary between weapons.
</p><p>Lets look at impact zones of <a href="/Arming_Sword" title="Arming Sword">Arming Sword</a>:
